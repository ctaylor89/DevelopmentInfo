Entering_a_bill

In QuickBooks Online (2026), entering a bill is used when you receive an invoice from a vendor that you will pay later. The 
process records the expense and creates an Accounts Payable (A/P) balance until you pay the bill.

Steps to Enter a Bill in QuickBooks Online
1. Open the Bill Entry Screen
	   Select + New (top left).
	   Under Vendors, select Bill.
2. Select the Vendor
In the Vendor field:
	Choose an existing vendor.
 Or if the vendor is new:
	Select + Add new
	Enter vendor details:
	Vendor name
	Email
	Address
	Payment terms (for example, Net 30)
*The due date is calculated based on the payment terms.

Paying_a_bill

In QuickBooks Online (2026), paying a bill is the process of "recording the payment" you make to a vendor for an outstanding bill. 
This action reduces your Accounts Payable (A/P) balance and records the expense as paid.

Steps to Pay a Bill in QuickBooks Online
1. Open the Pay Bill Screen
	   Select + New (top left).
	   Under Vendors, select Pay Bills.
2. Select the Vendor
In the Vendor field:
	Choose the vendor you want to pay.
3. Select the Bill
In the Bills section:
	Choose the bill you want to pay.
4. Enter Payment Details
In the Payment section:
	Enter the payment amount.
	Choose the payment method (e.g., Check, Credit Card).
	(Optional) Add a memo or note.
5. Save the Payment
	Select Save and close to record the payment.

Option 2: Pay the Bill
	Select + New
	Choose Pay Bills  
	Select the bill
	Choose the bank account
	Enter payment date
	Select Save and close
The payment creates:
	Debit: Accounts Payable            $300
	Credit: Checking Account           $300
*The payable is removed because the vendor has been paid.

Common Bookkeeping Mistake
	Do not enter a vendor invoice as a Check or Expense if you are paying later.

Correct workflow:
	Receive invoice → Enter Bill → Pay Bill later

Incorrect:
	Receive invoice → Enter Expense → Pay later

*The second method skips Accounts Payable and makes vendor balances inaccurate.

How do I enter a deposit into my bank account in QuickBooks Online?
1. Open the Deposit Screen
   Select + New (top left).
   Under Other, select Bank Deposit.

2. Select the Bank Account
In the Account field:
   Choose the bank account where the deposit will be made.
In the Account field:
   Choose the bank account where the deposit will be made.

3. Enter Deposit Information
In the Deposit section:
   Enter the date of the deposit.
   (Optional) Add a memo or note.
   In the Add funds to this deposit section:
      Choose the customer or vendor from the dropdown.
      Enter the amount of the deposit.
      (Optional) Add a description.

4. Save the Deposit
   Select Save and close to record the deposit.

The deposit will increase the balance of the selected bank account and may also affect income accounts, depending on the source of the funds.

Prompt: If I want to record a cash deposit that is made by the owner of the business into the business bank account, how should I categorize it in
QuickBooks Online?
A: To record a cash deposit made by the owner into the business bank account, you should categorize it as an 
"Owner's Contribution" or "Owner's Equity" in QuickBooks Online. This reflects the increase in the business's cash balance while 
also acknowledging the owner's investment in the business.

Prompt: If I want to record a cash deposit that is made by the owner of the business into the business bank account, what do I put
into the "Received From" field in QuickBooks Online?
A: In the "Received From" field, you should enter the owner's name or the name of the business (if it's a sole proprietorship) 
to accurately reflect the source of the funds being deposited.

How do get the owners name into the "Received From" drop down list for the field in QuickBooks Online?
A: To get the owner's name into the "Received From" drop-down list, you need to ensure that the owner's name is set up as a 
customer or vendor in QuickBooks Online. You can do this by following these steps:
1. Go to the Sales or Expenses menu.
2. Select Customers or Vendors.
3. Click on New customer or New vendor.
4. Enter the owner's name and any other relevant information.
5. Save the new customer or vendor.

Once the owner's name is set up, it should appear in the "Received From" drop-down list when recording a deposit.

Creating_a_Purchase_Order in QuickBooks Online
Creating a Purchase Order (PO) in QuickBooks Online is a straightforward way to track items or services you intend to buy from a vendor. Here is how to do it step-by-step:

Step 1: Open the Purchase Order Form
Click the + New button located at the top of the left navigation menu.

Under the Vendors column, select Purchase Order.

Step 2: Fill Out the Vendor and General Information
Vendor: Select your vendor from the dropdown menu. If they aren’t in your system yet, you can click Add New.

Mailing Address: This will auto-populate based on the vendor profile, but you can edit it if needed.

Ship To: If you are shipping directly to a customer or a specific location, choose the destination from the dropdown.

Purchase Order Date: Enter the date you are issuing the PO.

Ship Via: Specify the shipping method if applicable (e.g., FedEx, UPS).
